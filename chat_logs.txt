Question:
What is TF-IDF

Answer:
TF-IDF (Term Frequency-Inverse Document Frequency) is a technique used to convert textual data into numerical form.

*   It assigns each word a numerical weight that reflects its importance in distinguishing between fake and real news.
*   It highlights important words that contribute to the meaning of the text while reducing the impact of frequently used common words.
*   It combines two measures:
    *   **Term Frequency (TF):** Measures how frequently a word appears in a document.
    *   **Inverse Document Frequency (IDF):** Reduces the weight of commonly occurring words (like "the," "is," and "and") and increases the importance of less frequent but more meaningful words.

Question:
give one example

Answer:
Imagine you have two simple text snippets:

*   **Snippet 1:** "The **cat** sat on the mat."
*   **Snippet 2:** "The **dog** barked loudly."

Here's how TF-IDF conceptually assigns importance:

*   **Term Frequency (TF):**
    *   The word "the" appears once in Snippet 1 and once in Snippet 2.
    *   The word "**cat**" appears only once in Snippet 1.
    *   The word "**dog**" appears only once in Snippet 2.

*   **Inverse Document Frequency (IDF):**
    *   The word "the" is very common (appears in both snippets), so its IDF (and thus its overall TF-IDF weight) would be low.
    *   The word "**cat**" is less common (only in Snippet 1), so its IDF would be higher.
    *   The word "**dog**" is also less common (only in Snippet 2), so its IDF would be higher.

*   **TF-IDF Weight:** When TF and IDF are combined, words like "**cat**" in Snippet 1 and "**dog**" in Snippet 2 would receive a higher numerical weight than "the". This highlights "cat" as important for Snippet 1 and "dog" for Snippet 2, effectively distinguishing their topics.
